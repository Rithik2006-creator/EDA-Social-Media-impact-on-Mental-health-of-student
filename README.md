# 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis of Student Social Media Usage, Lifestyle, and Mental Health

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of a dataset containing information on **5,000 students**. The objective is to understand the relationships between **social media usage**, **academic habits**, **lifestyle factors**, and **mental health** through statistical analysis and data visualization.

The analysis explores how variables such as daily social media usage, study hours, sleep duration, physical activity, and stress levels are associated with students' mental health.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Understand students' social media usage patterns.
* Analyze platform preferences based on the purpose of use.
* Explore social media usage across different academic levels.
* Examine stress levels among students.
* Identify relationships between:

  * Social media usage
  * Study hours
  * Sleep duration
  * Physical activity
  * Mental health score
* Visualize trends and correlations using informative plots.

## 🔍 Key Findings

* Entertainment is the primary purpose of social media use across all academic levels.
* Facebook is the most preferred platform for networking, Instagram and TikTok dominate entertainment, Twitter is primarily used for news, while LinkedIn and YouTube are preferred for educational purposes.
* High school students are the largest heavy social media users compared to undergraduate and graduate students.
* High and very high stress levels are prevalent across all academic levels.
* Daily social media usage shows a strong negative relationship with study hours and mental health score.
* Higher social media usage is also associated with lower physical activity.
* Students with longer study hours generally have better mental health scores.
* Students who sleep longer and engage in more physical activity generally report lower stress levels.
